{% include block_heading.html title="Why SysON" %}
{% include block_cols.html col="6" icon_name="" icon_color="" title="" desc="SysML was created in 2005 as a standard for systems engineering to elevate the role of models as primary tools for communication and documentation.
With system complexity continuing to escalate exponentially, and Digital Engineering emerging as a pivotal pillar to address an ever-challenging world, SysMLv2 has been specified as the next-generation systems modeling language to improve precision, expressiveness, and usability." %} {% include block_cols.html col="6" icon_name="" icon_color="" title="" desc="SysON’s objective is to provide System Engineers with super easy access to this new standard, at minimal cost and great ease of use, with the guarantee of interoperability with other open-source MBSE tools notably Capella and Papyrus.
This will be achieved through three main means: the support of the SysMLv2 standard, the use of state-of-the-art web technologies, and an open-source approach." %}
